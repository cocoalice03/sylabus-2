COURSE PHILOSOPHY

The teaching approach is structured as follows:

THEORETICAL INTRODUCTION

Each session begins with a lecture and/or interactive presentation on the safety, physiology, and theoretical foundation associated with dry needling. Students will discuss and demonstrate safe dry needling technique and does associated with knee osteoarthritis. Students will also discuss the physiology of dry needling medicated analgesia and tissue repair.

SIMULATED PRACTICAL INTERVENTION IN PAIRS (OR GROUPS OF THREE)

After the theoretical introduction, students engage in practical exercises conducted in pairs or groups of three. This interactive teaching method allows each student to apply the techniques learned in a controlled and safe environment. Working in teams provides a concrete experience for each student, promoting exchange, observation, and immediate feedback.

COURSE RULES

The course will be organized in team-based work, structured in pairs of students or groups of three. It is essential that everyone demonstrates mutual respect and consideration, ensuring a safe and collaborative learning environment. This approach, based on simulated practices, will allow each student to gain practical experience with safe dry needling technique and dose of knee AO.
